If you don't know what Publix is, search it up or check out the Wikipedia [here.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Publix) I'll assume everyone reading will know about Walmart since it's much more widespread.

I recently walked into a Publix with a friend and was telling him about how much I love Publix.

I don't typically like big corporations because of their tendency to prioritize shareholder value over real value, but I think Publix is "one of the good guys".

I told my friend about how familiar Publix feels compared to shopping at Walmart. We were walking in to grab just one item, an act that I rarely ever pull off because of the sheer inefficiency, but it didn't feel that way because the Publix was in the very same plaza that my friend and I happened to find ourselves in. This wasn't accidental, and I'll talk about it in just a second.

We continued the conversation about what makes Publix distinct, and the topic has lingered on my mind. So, let's talk about it. Keep in mind that everything I say here about Publix is based on my own experiences with it in Miami, FL and nothing more.

The first thing is what I just mentioned. It felt convenient for us to head into Publix because of how it prioritizes a local presence. They seem to focus on building smaller stores as centerpieces of local plazas. Contrast that with Walmart, which tends to stand out with HUGE stores that are detached from any plaza (though they still end up being near plazas because of zoning, at least that's what I think is happening).

When you're in the store, the changes persist with the same theme. In Publix, the environment is tighter, evoking a feeling of coziness. In Walmart, the roof is far above you, evoking a feeling of intimidation. In Publix, the main color is green and all the adjacent colors are of the same warmth, creating a sense of comfort among shoppers (like myself). In Walmart, everything is white and ultra-bright, creating a detached, cold sentiment among shoppers (like myself). In Publix, there's light music played in the background and directions are plentiful, ensuring that you don't get lost. In Walmart, there is neither music (from my memory) nor good directions throughout the store.

The overall vibes that I get from Publix are "enjoy your shopping", whereas for Walmart it would be "get in, get all that you need, and get out". The first reflects quality whereas the other reflects quantity. And so despite Publix having higher grocery costs on average, there are many shoppers (like myself) that are willing to pay the price for the quality experience.

If only more people learned to reflect on small things like this...
