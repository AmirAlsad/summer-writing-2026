Put enough money, fame, or comfort in someone's face, and they'll throw *any* value out the window.

I recently watched a video from this guy named "GEN" (really great videos, especially for doomer type). Here's the video for reference: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YlSK_5fMSa4

It's got me thinking: what the heck are we doing? We know our devices are listening to us and that our every move online is being tracked, but no one ever talks about it. I'll be honest about it, I'm part of the "issue" (if we can even call it that).

My evaluation of this American (and really any Western) society has fallen so low that my dream is to become successful enough that I can detach from it. And I know many other share that same perspective. That matters are beyond salvageable, and that the best path forward is to leave it all behind and start a new life.

Things could have been another way, at least I think so. I'm not so sure it would've been from us, the people, acting differently though. From my knowledge and experience, the people generally can't stick to a long-term effort. They operate in trends. With short bursts of energy. Rarely every anything lasting and consistent. Another problem always manages to find it's way into the main context. The only exception that comes to mind is the mass movement against the tobacco industry, which suggests to me it's more likely a failure on the part of the tobacco industry rather than a pure success from the peoples' effort. That's a case study worth checking out.

Legislation seems to play a big role in these matters, but I can't seem to think of serious limitations that the government(s) have put on Big Tech AND that Big Tech hasn't simply side-steped. The EU seems to have done something impactful since it seems that I'm always reading some disclaimer about functionality in the EU, but I'm not so well-read on the topic.

In the past few days, I saw a video on LinkedIn for some software that you can pass an image into, and it will geo-locate that image from the relevant pixels (if any, of course). It works. It wasn't a sci-fi clip. It was a product demo. This isn't that crazy privacy-wise (since our phones already have GPS), but it still lives in my mind. Perhaps a more pressing development (or at least it seems to me like a recent development) is WiFi sensing, where your WiFi signal can map out the layout of the building and positions of the people within it. So, anyone with access to your WiFi (which isn't difficult to break into) and a well-trained neural network can know exactly where you are and what you are doing (yes, the fidelity can be good enough to observe motion) in your own home. No visuals needed. Perhaps the future of robotics involves using a home's WiFi network instead of computer vision. In fact, I wouldn't be surprised if there's a startup working on this right now.

Oh well...
