I'm copping out again from writing a piece by referencing one of my previous works (see "Revolutionary Memory in Miami"). This one is about the credit hour as a unit and how it shaped the American education system.

I created the podcast as part of a class at MIT called "Producing Podcasts". We learned, as the name of the class would suggest, how to produce a podcast.

I've been playing with this idea of starting an actual podcast, though I've struggled to find the will to commit to it, and I'll likely continue to struggle for the near future. BUT, *if I were to start one*, I would name it **Stupid Standards** and talk about the standards behind the bureaucracies of the world. Why do I like this idea? Put simply, I despise bureaucracies. For their inefficiency and for their ridiculous limitations.

Reflecting on my work, I've realized it's a decent case study for how entire systems can be built on a single idea, even if that idea isn't necessarily a great one. I'll aim to write another piece soon to reflect on the work in a case-study fashion.
