It was the early portion of the night. I found myself lying on the floor of the MIT [Musalla.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musalla) I had been feeling uneasy. My life was great, all praise be to God, but I wasn't satisfied. I was sleeping too much and not working out. Then, I realized. I had been optimizing for productivity. It was productivity in "finishing the task" that prevented me from sleeping at a consistent hour. That same inconsistency in sleep caused me to oversleep. And the next day, given that I had already "lost time" from oversleeping, I reasoned that I didn't have enough time for a full workout, and so it would be more optimal for me to get ahead on my work. I was behind before the day even started.

In hindsight, I think "what the heck was I thinking/doing?" But being conscious in a world of distractions is difficult enough. It's even more difficult to then reflect on all that we notice and figure out what needs to be done. Then, on top of figuring out what needs to be done, we must take action. A friction-full series of events when once can alternatively spend their entire day constantly stimulated by social media, video games, and all other forms of content.

Optimizing your life around one thing means that you must accept the tradeoffs. If you cannot or do not accept the tradeoffs, then you should immediately kill such optimization until you get your priorities straght. For me, that means getting 7 hours of sleep, working out on a daily basis, and a few hours of solitude to think deeply and write. The rest of my day can be grueling work. Looking back, the most fulfilling time of my life was when I consistently got these 3 things and some work to keep my mind busy (in a way that's tied to the real world, unlike a full day of pondering).

Until next time, ✌️.
