It's frightening. It's frightening to write this piece, because writing this piece is the start of a much longer "expose" of my thoughts and ideas. It's not like I'm confessing to some horrific crime, but I am opening the door to something more intimate: revealing how I think about and view the world. Perhaps I'm being overly dramatic.

So, what is this site? Well, I'll be writing a piece for every day of the summer, which starts today and ends when the Fall semester begins (or perhaps something more significant). This site is where I'll be hosting said pieces.

Now, why am I doing this? There are a few reasons.

First, in my personal writing, I've found that there's immense benefit in writing out one's thoughts and ideas. The very nature of writing forces you to stick to what you've said, as opposed to verbal communication where you can say one thing and absolutely contradict it a minute later without realizing what you've said. In other words, writing forces you to get your thoughts in order. That's the primary benefit that I can speak to. I haven't thought much about any others if they do exist.

Second, a challenge like this one requires consistency, and the requirement for consistency pressures me (in a positive sense) to structure my days properly. You can think of life as some physical system and this challenge as some constraint. As with most physical systems (I'm tempted to make an absolute claim here, but that's probably a foolish thing to do), if you're trying to evoke a particular behavior, you would add constraints to the systems behavior in a way that's conducive for your desired behavior to show up more frequently. What are the behaviors that this constraint is supposed to help me with? Obviously writing more, but also making good use of my mornings, which is when I prefer to do my writing.

Third, I've realized the importance of creating a personal brand, and this challenge is a stepping stone for me to begin that journey. The other two reasons don't explain why I'm doing this challenge publicly. I could've just done it for myself in my private journals, but this reason convinced me otherwise. Now, it's clear that I'm not carrying out the "normal" content creation that would go alongside creating a personal brand. That's simply because I cannot bring myself to record videos and post them as short-form content (for a couple of reasons that we won't explore in this piece, but maybe a future one). Writing, on the other hand, seems to be a more plausible approach to content creation.

Well, I guess that marks the first of many. Until next time, ✌️.
