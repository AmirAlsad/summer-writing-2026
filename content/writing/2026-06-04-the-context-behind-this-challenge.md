## Preface

This challenge is starting to get difficult... Just 3 days in.

Writing every day, on it's own, is a difficult proposition. You have to consistently set aside time to share your thoughts on or experience with some topic. If your mind isn't already full of thoughts or experiences, then you must force it so. But, at least there's flexibility in that you can write as much or as little as you want. You could ramble on about your day, or you could write your raw thoughts on some topic without any substantiation for your claims.

In my case, I'm sort of "playing on hard mode" since every single piece must surface to the web. So, I don't just have to write daily about some topic. I have to do so with a much higher bar for quality. After all, my public image is on the line.

For context, I haven't shared this challenge with anyone at the time of this writing (I'll get to it, I promise). I could just switch things up and play it off like nothing happened. But I gain nothing in that path of action but comfort, and comfort wasn't my intention behind starting this challenge. My intention was to struggle and grow through that struggle.

Anyways, I should probably get to the main topic...


## The Short Explanation

I want to "make it big" when it comes to business and influence, but I don't feel comfortable engaging in systems that I think are more harmful than they are helpful. So, instead of content creation in your typical "influencer" fashion, I figured I could just write for now and sort out the rest later.


## The Long Explanation

I'm not a fan of social media. I'm firm on that. I haven't fully worked out my thoughts, but I plan to read the Anxious Generation by Jonathan Haidt soon, so I might write a dedicated piece on the matter. So, from here, we can label my perspective of it as "evil." It'll come in handy in just a bit.

Now, for most of my time spent thinking about social media, I never considered extracting any genuine value from it. In other words, I couldn't see anyone benefitting anything from it besides entertainment or fame (only for the big guys), and I had no need for entertainment nor fame, hence there being no genuine value. But times are changing.

A few months back, I read Alex Hormozi's $100 Million Leads book, and he makes a simple, yet strong case for content creation. It's the only lead source that combines trust (warm outreach) with scale (one to many). Not just that, but it's an appreciating asset that takes time to build, and so it's only reasonable that, if anyone wants to compete in the world of business and isn't already producing content, they should start *now.* Of course, there was much more to the argument that entrepreneurs should be producing content beyond what I've written here.

I didn't want to accept it. My opinions against social media emerged as a feeling of bitterness. I knew he was right. I should be producing content. But I didn't want to, not because I couldn't produce any (even if it was the case, it's just another skill that has to be learnt), but because I had to now contribute to a system that I had previously labeled as "evil." So, my perspective shifted to "necessary evil." Beyond this realization that I'll eventually need to do this, theres an anxiety of posting anything online (unless it's extremely well-polished), primarily because of that middle-school instinct to "not be cringe."

A few months go by and I'm still not producing content. I'm at another event and I'm talking to the head of social media at Wix. I tell her how I understand that I should be producing content, but that I'm uncomfortable with making frequent posts throughout my day or recording videos (i.e. what I think content on Instagram or Facebook looks like). She tells me something along the lines of "ok, and?" to which I respond with confusion. It's then explained that I don't need to be producing content for Instagram or Facebook. I could produce content in the manner that I find most engaging, even if it's at a low frequency, but I had to start. That was the clarification I needed.

I made the blunder of conflating content creation with social media, but once that was sorted out, the only thing left in the way was actually executing. It was then that I figured "okay, I like writing about the things that interest me, so let me start a blog." Of course, I still delay the matter with the excuse of "life circumstances", but we've finally made it to where we are. Now, the reason I'm doing this challenge and not just blogging when I feel like it is for commitment. It'll help me, hopefully, consistently put out lots of content by the end of summer.

Until next time, ✌️.
