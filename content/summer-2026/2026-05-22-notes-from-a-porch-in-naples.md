It is hot in the way Naples is hot, which is to say the air has opinions. By eleven you are not so much sweating as being slowly compressed. I sat on the porch all morning trying to read and managed only to turn pages.

There were lemons. There are always lemons here. The woman across the street has a tree that drops them onto the sidewalk and she does not pick them up — she lets them sit until somebody asks, and then she gives the asker three, by hand, with a small, irritated benediction.

Down the block, four men have been playing scopa at the same plastic table since I arrived. I do not know if they have moved. I do not know if they sleep there. Today one of them slapped a card down hard enough that a cat fled from under the table I had not known was occupied.

I came to write a long essay this morning. Instead I wrote this. The day insisted. The week will insist on other things. I am learning, slowly, to let them.
