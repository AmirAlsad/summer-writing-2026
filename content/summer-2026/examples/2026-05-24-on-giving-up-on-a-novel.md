title: "On giving up on a novel"
description: "The one I had been writing for two years, and what was left when I stopped."

I stopped working on the novel on a Tuesday, which is exactly the wrong day to stop working on anything — too late in the week to feel decisive, too early to feel like rest. I closed the document, moved it into a folder called *old*, and went to make coffee. By the time the kettle clicked off I knew I was not going back.

It had been two years. About sixty thousand words. A plot that had collapsed in the second act and never fully stood back up. I do not think it was a bad novel, exactly — it was a novel that had not figured out what it wanted to be, and I had been trying to figure that out for it, which is a thing you cannot do.

What surprised me was how clean the giving-up felt. I had imagined, for years, that the day I quit would be a small grief — that I would mourn the version of myself who had been carrying the manuscript around like a second wallet. Instead it was a relief that bordered on indecent. I went for a walk. I bought a sandwich. I noticed the sky.

The next morning I started something else. I do not know what it is yet. I do know it is not the novel. That is enough information to begin.
