The porch faces the water and gets direct sun until about four in the afternoon, at which point the umbrella pine on the right side of the garden takes over and the temperature drops six degrees in under a minute. I have timed this. It is one of the more reliable events in my life right now.

The lemons here are the size of softballs and taste like someone decided to make lemon more serious. I have been eating them with salt, which is not something I would have done at home, but which feels correct in this context. Everything feels slightly more correct in this context.

There is a bar at the end of the street where four men between the ages of seventy and eighty play scopa every afternoon from two until whenever. I have watched them for three days now. They do not talk much. They slap the cards down with a conviction I find genuinely moving — the conviction of people who have been doing the same thing together long enough that they no longer need to perform enjoyment. They just have it.

I have been thinking about what it takes to get to that. The years of showing up. The willingness to be a regular somewhere, with the same people, at the same table, until the table starts to feel like yours without anyone having to say so.

I do not think I am there yet with anything. But I am watching.
