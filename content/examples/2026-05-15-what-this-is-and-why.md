I have always wanted to write every day, and I have always found a reason not to. The reasons are reliable. They show up like weather. So this summer I am going to do it anyway — every morning of the summer, one piece each, posted before I get to second-guess it.

Some of these will be essays. A few will be fiction. Most will be somewhere in between, the way half my notebook is. I am not going to publish things that are finished. I am going to publish things that are *done for today*, which is a different bar, and a more honest one.

If you read along, here is what I will ask of you: read it the way you read a postcard — quickly, generously, without looking for the seams. If you write something back, send it. If you do not, that is also fine. The publishing is the practice. The reading is a kindness.

A new piece every morning, all summer. The site goes live one day at a time — there is no archive of future posts, no preview, no calendar to scroll ahead in. You only see what the sun has already passed over.

See you tomorrow.
